Session 11 - December 17, 2023

Session Summary

In-Game Date: Unknown
Dimension: 1

Key Events:

  • Intense Combat Resolutions: The battle with Windy and his wind manipulation continues. Templar manages to hit him with a shot that sends him to the ground, dissipating the whirlwind. Despite a failed illusion attempt by Manifester, Windy relocates using his wind powers.
  • Unexpected Interruptions and Public Sightings: Amid the chaos, Mantis Shrimp receives a call from his lawyer about Deadpool looking for him, and encounters tourists led by Mark Seargent, who dismisses Mantis Shrimp as animatronic to his tour group.
  • Dramatic Rescues: Mantis Shrimp performs a heroic act by catching the falling Celica, proving to the onlookers that he is indeed real. Manifester and Templar engage with more foes in a nearby building, encountering a woman chained up and a hostile entity known as Bulltoad.
  • Confrontations with New Enemies: The battle escalates with the appearance of new adversaries. Bulltoad attacks Manifester, leading to a violent confrontation where Templar eventually incapacitates Bulltoad. Meanwhile, an invisible woman and a concrete-like creature engage the heroes, adding layers of complexity to the fight.
  • Dynamic Heroics and Tactics: As the fights unfold, the team employs a mix of physical combat, psychic powers, and tactical maneuvers. Templar and Mantis Shrimp deal with the concrete creature, ultimately sending him crashing through the ceiling.
  • Resolving Hostage Situations: After the battles, they attend to the chained woman, Carmelita, ensuring her safety and managing the situation without fully removing her restraints.
  • Strategic Escape and Disguise: The session concludes with the team camouflaging their vehicles to pass through a controlled area undetected, leveraging the confusion and their abilities to escape potential further confrontations.
  • Clues to Further Adventures: Inside the Celica, they find a costume catalogue addressed to a mansion on Ward Parkway, hinting at their next destination or link in their ongoing adventures.

Note: This session is filled with action, strategy, and moments of humor and heroics that define the characters and their interactions with the world around them. The narrative continues to intertwine public perception, superhero antics, and personal stakes, driving forward the campaign’s overarching themes and setting up new avenues for exploration and conflict.

Manifest teleports into the other building, second floor. Sees a woman wearing a harness with chain attached. They hear the engine of the crane give out. Car is falling. Mantis runs under and catches the car. People on the bus say “He’s not animatronic! Mantis Shrimp is real.” The tour bus quickly drives away with Mark grumbling.

Manifester sees Human-Toad-Bull combo. He says “You shouldn’t have come here. Now Bulltoad is gonna have to kick your ass!” Manifester blasts Bulltoad with psychic blast. Bulltoad comes bounding towards Manifester, jiggly and gores Manifester, pitching him out window onto awning. “Woman, see what you made me do? Carmelita you know I only keep you chained up because of certain masculine insecurities.” Templar shoots Bulltoad, and takes him down to 0; frog goo splatters all over Manifester. “You killed him! You killed him, you horrible monster!” Mantis puts Automatic Man into the Celica gently, covers him with a jacket, then jet-packs to where he saw Windy-Armor flee. Energy blast from invisible woman flies past Templar, who is now visible. Manifester psychic blasts at woman but misses. Bulltoad is breathing but incapacitated.

Templar runs towards woman, she blasts at him but misses again, again impacting the building. Mantis doesn’t see, but hear and feel wind picking up. Mantis has no idea where he is. Manifester attempts to make an illusion of Khaki Tacs storming the building, but fails. Templar tackles her. Zip ties her. Mantis hears a rumbling sound but not soon enough. Gets sucker-punched, 15 damage, flies skittering across the floor. Whoever punched him descends from the ceiling, looks like living concrete in human form. Mantis heads towards new threat, shrinking down. (+6 defense) Manifester misses with another psychic blast at concrete guy. Templar shoots him, but bullet appears to bounce off him. Concrete says “You’re gonna have to do better than that,” and smacks the ground and damages the ground. Mantis and Templar both fall through floor to the level below. Concrete reaches up and absorbs himself up into the ceiling. Mantis flies up through the hole in the floor back to the second floor. Manifester psychic blasts concrete guy for 1 stun dmg. Templar throws out rope and climbs back up to second floor.

Concrete swings at Mantis and misses. Mantis swings back, unshrinking, uppercutting and smashing concrete up and out through the ceiling and much later, there’s the sound of concrete falling. Situation resolved; they set Carmelita back up and reassure her the Bulltoad is ok. They loosen but do not remove her bounds. Now they need to get past the Khaki Tacts on the outskirts of the No-Go Zone. Manifester gets into the Celica and the CD player, scrambled by the magnet, now plays “Give It Up” by KC & The Sunshine Band. Manifester then disguises vehicles as Khaki Tac vehicles, and they slip past without issue. In Celica they find a fancy costume catalogue, addressed to a house on Ward Parkway—basically a mansion.
